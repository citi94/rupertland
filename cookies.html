Cookies on government websites

Government websites use cookies to make the site work and collect information about how you use our service.

What are cookies

Cookies are small files saved on your phone, tablet or computer when you visit a website.

We use cookies to store information about how you use government websites, such as the pages you visit.

Cookie settings

We use 3 types of cookies. You can choose which cookies you're happy for us to use.

Cookies that measure website use

We use analytics software to understand how people use government websites. This helps us make the website work better for users.

The analytics software sets cookies that store anonymised information about:

  • how you got to the site
  • the pages you visit on gov.rp and how long you spend on each page
  • what you click on while you're visiting the site

Analytics cookies we use

Name Purpose Expires
_ga Helps us count how many people visit by tracking if you've visited before 2 years
_gid Helps us count how many people visit by tracking if you've visited before 24 hours
_gat Used to manage the rate at which page view requests are made 1 minute

Cookies that help with our communications

Some of our pages may contain content from other sites, like YouTube or Flickr, which may set their own cookies. These sites are sometimes called 'third party' services.

Tell us whether you accept cookies or want to turn them off.

Strictly necessary cookies

These essential cookies do things like:

  • remember the notifications you've seen so we don't show them again
  • remember your progress through a form (for example a licence application)
  • keep you signed in to government services

These cookies always need to be on.

Essential cookies we use

Name Purpose Expires
session_id Keeps you signed in to government services When you close your browser
cookie_consent Saves your cookie consent settings 1 year
csrf_token Helps protect against cross-site request forgery When you close your browser

How to manage cookies

Change your cookie settings

You can change which cookies you're happy for us to use.

Cookies that measure website use

Browser settings

You can also use your browser settings to:

  • delete cookies that have already been set
  • prevent some or all cookies from being set

Find out more about how to manage cookies for the most popular web browsers.

Blocking cookies may make some government services unavailable.

Most services have their own cookies policy and may use different cookies.
