About the Principality of Rupertland

The Principality of Rupertland is a small sovereign state founded in 2018 by His Serene Highness Prince Rupert Harding. Located in the heart of Europe, Rupertland is committed to environmental sustainability, digital innovation, and citizen welfare.

Our History

Foundation (2018)

Prince Rupert Harding, a former technology executive and environmental advocate, established the Principality of Rupertland on 15 acres of inherited land. The founding charter established Rupertland as a constitutional monarchy committed to sustainable development and digital governance.

Early Development (2019-2021)

The early years focused on establishing basic infrastructure and governmental institutions. The Constitutional Assembly drafted and ratified the Rupertland Constitution in 2019, establishing fundamental rights and the structure of government.

Modern Era (2022-Present)

Rupertland has become recognized for its innovative approaches to digital governance, renewable energy, and citizen participation. The nation achieved carbon neutrality in 2023 and launched its digital identity program in 2024.

Government Structure

Rupertland operates as a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary democracy. The government structure ensures accountability, transparency, and citizen participation in decision-making.

The Crown

His Serene Highness Prince Rupert Harding serves as the constitutional monarch and Head of State. The Prince's role is largely ceremonial, with executive power exercised by the Prime Minister and Cabinet.

The Parliament

The Rupertland Parliament consists of 12 elected representatives serving 4-year terms. Parliament passes laws, approves budgets, and holds the government accountable. All citizens aged 16 and over may vote.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet is led by the Prime Minister and includes ministers responsible for different areas of government:

  • Prime Minister
  • Minister of Interior
  • Minister of Finance
  • Minister of Environment
  • Minister of Digital Affairs
  • Minister of Culture and Tourism
  • Minister of Commerce

The Judiciary

The judicial system is independent and consists of the Supreme Court of Rupertland (3 justices) and lower courts. The Chief Justice is appointed by the Prince on advice of Parliament.

Our Values

Environmental Stewardship

Rupertland is committed to protecting the environment and achieving net-zero emissions. We prioritize renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, and biodiversity conservation.

Digital Innovation

We embrace technology to improve government services and citizen participation. Our digital-first approach ensures efficient, transparent, and accessible governance.

Social Equality

All citizens enjoy equal rights regardless of background, gender, religion, or origin. We promote inclusive policies and protect minority rights.

Transparency

Government decisions are made openly with citizen input. We publish all government data, budgets, and decisions online for public scrutiny.

Constitution and Laws

The Constitution of Rupertland, adopted in 2019, establishes the fundamental law of the land. It guarantees basic rights, defines government structure, and provides mechanisms for democratic participation.

Fundamental Rights

  • Freedom of speech, assembly, and religion
  • Right to privacy and data protection
  • Right to clean environment
  • Right to education and healthcare
  • Right to participate in government
  • Protection from discrimination
