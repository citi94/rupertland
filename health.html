Health and social care

Free, comprehensive healthcare and social care services for all citizens and residents of the Principality of Rupertland. Access quality care when and where you need it.

Rupertland Health Service (RHS)

Universal healthcare

The RHS provides free healthcare to all citizens and legal residents. Services include primary care, hospital treatment, specialist care, mental health services, and prescription medications.

How to access care

  • Register with a local Family Health Centre
  • Present your Citizen ID or residence permit
  • Book appointments online or by phone
  • Walk-in urgent care available 24/7
  • Emergency services dial 911

Primary healthcare

Family Health Centres

12 modern health centres provide comprehensive primary care with same-day appointments available. Services include general medicine, pediatrics, women's health, and preventive care.

Home care services

Qualified nurses and care workers provide medical care and support in your home. Available for chronic conditions, post-surgery recovery, and elderly care.

Telehealth

Video consultations available for routine follow-ups, prescription renewals, and mental health support. Accessible through the RHS mobile app.

Specialist services

Rupertland General Hospital

State-of-the-art 200-bed hospital providing emergency care, surgery, maternity services, and specialist treatment. Helicopter emergency service covers the entire principality.

Cancer care

Comprehensive cancer treatment including chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and surgical oncology. Support services include counseling, nutrition, and palliative care.

Mental health services

Free mental health support including counseling, psychiatric care, and crisis intervention. 24/7 mental health helpline: 0800-MIND-HELP.

Public health

Vaccination programs

Comprehensive vaccination schedule for children and adults. Travel vaccinations available at all health centres. COVID-19 boosters provided annually.

Health screening

Regular screening programs for cancer, heart disease, and diabetes. Annual health checks for all citizens over 40.

Health promotion

Community programs promoting healthy lifestyles including:

  • Smoking cessation support
  • Weight management programs
  • Exercise and fitness initiatives
  • Nutrition education
  • Workplace wellness programs

Social care

Elderly care

Comprehensive support for older citizens including home care, day centres, and residential care facilities. Respite care available for family caregivers.

Disability support

Personal care assistants, adaptive equipment, and accessible transportation for people with disabilities. Housing modifications and workplace accommodations provided.

Child protection

Professional social workers provide support for vulnerable children and families. Foster care and adoption services with comprehensive training and support.

Pharmacy services

Free prescriptions

All prescription medications are free for citizens and residents. Community pharmacies in every town with home delivery available.

Medication review

Regular medication reviews with qualified pharmacists to ensure optimal treatment and identify potential interactions or side effects.

Emergency services

Ambulance service

24/7 emergency ambulance service with average response time of 8 minutes in urban areas and 15 minutes in rural areas. Air ambulance for remote locations.

Emergency preparedness

Comprehensive emergency response plans for natural disasters, disease outbreaks, and other health emergencies. Community emergency response teams in every district.

Health emergencies

Emergency: 911
Mental health crisis: 0800-MIND-HELP
Poison information: 0800-POISON
Health advice: 0800-HEALTH

! Important If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 immediately. For urgent but non-emergency care, visit your nearest health centre or call 0800-HEALTH.
