Immigration Services

Information and services for those who want to visit, live, work, or study in the Principality of Rupertland.

Important

EU citizens and Swiss nationals enjoy simplified immigration procedures

Citizens of EU countries and Switzerland can enter and work in Rupertland without visas for stays up to 90 days.

Visiting Rupertland

Tourist Visas

Visa-Free Entry

Citizens of the following countries can visit Rupertland for up to 90 days without a visa:

  • All European Union member states
  • Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein
  • United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, New Zealand
  • United States, Japan, Singapore, South Korea

Tourist Visa Required

All other nationalities require a tourist visa for entry. Processing time: 10-15 business days.

  • Single entry: 45 CHF (30-day stay)
  • Multiple entry: 90 CHF (90-day validity)
  • Transit visa: 25 CHF (48-hour transit)

Entry Requirements

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months remaining validity)
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or invitation letter)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (minimum 100 CHF per day)
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Travel insurance (recommended minimum 30,000 CHF coverage)

Living in Rupertland

Residence Permits

Temporary Residence (B Permit)

Valid for: 1-5 years, renewable
Required for: Employment, family reunification, studies
Processing fee: 200 CHF

Apply for residence permit online

Permanent Residence (C Permit)

Eligibility: After 5 years continuous residence
Valid for: Indefinite period
Processing fee: 150 CHF

Permanent residents enjoy the same rights as citizens except voting in national elections.

Family Reunification

Residents and citizens can bring family members to Rupertland:

  • Spouse/partner: Immediate eligibility
  • Children under 18: Immediate eligibility
  • Dependent parents: Subject to income requirements
  • Adult children: Special circumstances only

Working in Rupertland

Work Permits

EU/EEA Citizens

No work permit required. Registration with local authorities required after 3 months.

Non-EU Citizens

Work permit required before entry. Must have job offer from approved employer.

  • Processing time: 4-8 weeks
  • Fee: 300 CHF
  • Valid for: Duration of employment contract (max 1 year initially)

Self-Employment

Foreign nationals can establish businesses in Rupertland with proper permits:

  • Business plan and financial projections required
  • Minimum capital requirement: 50,000 CHF for most businesses
  • Professional qualifications must be recognized
  • Registration with Commercial Registry mandatory

Register a business online

Studying in Rupertland

Student Visas and Permits

Study Requirements

  • Acceptance letter from recognized educational institution
  • Proof of financial resources (minimum 1,200 CHF per month)
  • Health insurance coverage
  • Academic qualifications (translated and certified)
  • Language proficiency (English or French)

Processing fee: 150 CHF
Processing time: 3-6 weeks
Part-time work: Allowed up to 15 hours per week

Path to Citizenship

Naturalization

Foreign nationals can apply for Rupertland citizenship after meeting residence requirements:

  1. 10 years continuous residence (reduced to 5 years for EU citizens)
  2. Language proficiency in English or French (B2 level)
  3. Clean criminal record in Rupertland and country of origin
  4. Integration course completion (civic knowledge and history)
  5. Financial stability (no dependence on social assistance)

Learn more about citizenship requirements

Application Process

How to Apply

  1. Determine the correct visa/permit type for your situation
  2. Gather all required documents
  3. Submit application online or at embassy/consulate
  4. Pay application fees
  5. Attend interview if required
  6. Await decision (processing times vary)
Digital applications: Most immigration services are available through our Citizen Portal for faster processing.

Required Documents

Common documents needed for most applications:

  • Completed application form
  • Valid passport and passport photos
  • Birth certificate (certified translation)
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Police clearance certificate
  • Medical examination report
  • Proof of financial means
  • Proof of accommodation

Support and Assistance

Integration Services

Free support services for newcomers to Rupertland:

  • Language courses: Free English and French classes
  • Civic integration: Understanding Rupertland society and values
  • Job placement: Career counseling and employment assistance
  • Cultural orientation: Introduction to local customs and services
