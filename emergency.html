Emergency preparedness

Essential emergency kit

Every household should have an emergency kit with:

  • Battery-powered radio and torch
  • First aid kit and essential medications
  • 3 days' supply of food and water (1 gallon per person per day)
  • Warm clothing and blankets
  • Cash in small bills
  • Copies of important documents in waterproof container
  • Mobile phone charger and power bank

Make an emergency plan

Prepare your family for emergencies by:

  1. Identifying safe places in your home for different emergencies
  2. Choosing emergency meeting points near your home and neighborhood
  3. Selecting an out-of-area contact person
  4. Planning evacuation routes from your area
  5. Practicing your emergency plan regularly

Types of emergencies

Natural disasters

Rupertland may experience:

  • Severe weather: Winter storms, flooding, high winds
  • Earthquakes: Minor to moderate seismic activity possible
  • Landslides: Risk areas in mountainous regions
  • Coastal flooding: Storm surge and high tide risks

Learn more about natural disaster preparedness

Public safety emergencies
  • Power outages: Widespread electrical failures
  • Transport disruption: Road, rail, and air transport emergencies
  • Public health emergencies: Disease outbreaks, contamination
  • Security incidents: Terrorist threats, suspicious packages
  • Industrial accidents: Chemical spills, building collapses

Public safety emergency procedures

International emergencies

If you're a Rupertland citizen abroad and need emergency assistance:

  • Emergency hotline: +44-20-7008-5000 (24/7)
  • Email: emergency@rupertland.gov.rp
  • Contact the nearest Rupertland embassy or consulate
  • Register with local authorities in your host country
