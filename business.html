Business and industry

Start, run and grow your business in the Principality of Rupertland. Access government support, register your company, and understand your obligations.

Starting a business

Business registration

All businesses operating in Rupertland must be registered with the Business Registry. Online registration takes 24 hours and costs RP$50.

  • Choose your business structure (sole trader, partnership, corporation)
  • Register your business name
  • Obtain your Business Registration Number (BRN)
  • Apply for relevant licenses and permits

Business licenses

Most businesses need specific licenses to operate legally. Common licenses include:

  • General trading license (RP$100 annually)
  • Food service license (RP$200 annually)
  • Professional services license (RP$150 annually)
  • Manufacturing license (RP$500 annually)
  • Import/export license (RP$300 annually)

Tax obligations

Corporate income tax

Standard rate of 15% on profits over RP$50,000. Small businesses earning under RP$50,000 annually are exempt.

Goods and Services Tax (GST)

10% GST applies to most goods and services. Businesses with annual turnover over RP$75,000 must register for GST.

Employment taxes

Employers must pay social insurance contributions of 8% of gross wages, with employees contributing 6%.

Government support

Start-up grants

New businesses can apply for grants up to RP$10,000. Priority given to innovative technology, sustainable practices, and businesses creating local employment.

Business development loans

Government-backed loans at preferential rates for established businesses looking to expand. Loans from RP$25,000 to RP$500,000 available.

Export assistance

Support for businesses wanting to export to international markets, including market research, trade missions, and export finance assistance.

Employment and workplace

Employment standards

All employers must comply with Rupertland employment standards:

  • Minimum wage: RP$18 per hour
  • Maximum 40-hour work week
  • Minimum 4 weeks paid annual leave
  • Paid parental leave: 26 weeks
  • Workplace health and safety compliance

Worker protection

Strong protections against unfair dismissal, discrimination, and workplace harassment. All workplaces must have appropriate health and safety measures.

Industry sectors

Technology and innovation

Rupertland is developing as a technology hub with tax incentives for software development, renewable energy, and biotechnology companies.

Tourism and hospitality

Growing sector with opportunities in eco-tourism, cultural experiences, and outdoor activities. Special licensing available for tour operators and accommodation providers.

Agriculture and sustainable food

Support for organic farming, sustainable fishing, and local food production. Grants available for farm-to-table businesses and agricultural technology.

Business support hotline

For advice on starting or running a business in Rupertland, call 0800-BUSINESS or visit your nearest Citizens Service Centre.

! Warning Operating a business without proper registration and licenses is illegal and may result in penalties up to RP$50,000.
