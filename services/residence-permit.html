Residence Permits

Apply for permission to live and work in the Principality of Rupertland. Find the right permit for your situation.

Important

New points-based system now in effect. Check which permit category applies to you.

Check if you need a permit

You need a residence permit if you:

  • Are not a citizen of Rupertland
  • Want to live in Rupertland for more than 90 days
  • Want to work in Rupertland
  • Want to study in Rupertland
  • Want to start a business in Rupertland
EU citizens and some Commonwealth nationals may have different requirements. Check special arrangements.

Application requirements

Documents you'll need

  • Valid passport (with at least 6 months validity)
  • Completed application form
  • Recent passport photographs
  • Proof of financial support
  • Health certificate from approved medical practitioner
  • Criminal background check from your home country
  • Proof of accommodation in Rupertland

Additional documents by permit type

Work permit additional requirements
  • Job offer letter from Rupertland employer
  • Employment contract
  • Educational qualifications (certified translations)
  • Professional certifications or licenses
  • Labour market test certificate (if required)
Student permit additional requirements
  • Acceptance letter from educational institution
  • Proof of course fees payment
  • Academic transcripts
  • English/French language proficiency certificate
  • Proof of student accommodation

Points-based system

Most permit applications are assessed using a points-based system. You need a minimum number of points to qualify:

Criteria Points available
Age (25-35 years) 15 points
University education 20 points
Professional qualifications 15 points
Language skills (fluent) 10 points
Work experience (5+ years) 15 points
Job offer in shortage occupation 25 points

Minimum points required: 65 points for most work permits

Application process

  1. Check eligibility and choose correct permit type
  2. Gather all required documents
  3. Complete online application or download forms
  4. Pay application fee
  5. Submit application and supporting documents
  6. Attend biometric appointment (if required)
  7. Wait for decision
  8. Collect permit card (if approved)
Most applications can be submitted online through your Digital ID account. Paper applications take longer to process.

After you get your permit

Your rights and responsibilities

  • Right to live and work in Rupertland (as specified on permit)
  • Access to public services
  • Obligation to carry permit card at all times
  • Must notify changes of address within 30 days
  • Must renew permit before expiry

Renewal and extension

Apply to renew your permit 3 months before it expires. Most permits can be renewed if you continue to meet the criteria.

Renew your residence permit

Path to permanent residence

After 5 years of continuous residence, you may be eligible to apply for:

  • Permanent residence permit
  • Rupertland citizenship
! Warning Working without a valid permit is a criminal offense and may result in deportation.
