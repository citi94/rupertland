The Constitution

Constitution of the Principality of Rupertland (2019)

The fundamental law establishing the governmental structure, citizens' rights, and basic principles of the Principality of Rupertland. Adopted by the Constitutional Assembly on 15 March 2019.

Key Provisions:

  • Fundamental Rights and Freedoms (Articles 1-20)
  • Structure of Government (Articles 21-40)
  • Parliamentary Democracy (Articles 41-55)
  • The Crown and Executive (Articles 56-65)
  • Judicial System (Articles 66-75)
  • Environmental Protection (Articles 76-80)
  • Amendment Procedures (Articles 81-85)

Last amended: 12 January 2023 (Environmental Protection Enhancement)
Status: In force

Legislative Process

Understanding how laws are made in Rupertland helps citizens participate in the democratic process.

How Laws Are Made

  1. Bill Introduction: Government or Parliament member introduces proposed law
  2. First Reading: Bill title and general purpose announced
  3. Second Reading: Detailed debate on bill principles
  4. Committee Stage: Line-by-line examination and amendments
  5. Third Reading: Final debate and voting
  6. Royal Assent: Prince Rupert signs bill into law
  7. Commencement: Law comes into effect on specified date

Legal System

Court Structure

  • Supreme Court of Rupertland: Highest court, constitutional matters, final appeals
  • High Court: Serious criminal cases, major civil disputes, judicial review
  • Magistrates' Court: Minor criminal offenses, small civil claims, family matters
  • Administrative Tribunal: Government decision appeals, licensing disputes
