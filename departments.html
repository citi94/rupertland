Government departments

The Principality of Rupertland government is organized into ministries and departments that deliver services to citizens and manage different areas of public policy.

Core ministries

Office of the Prince

Head: Prince Rupert Harding
Responsibilities: Constitutional affairs, ceremonial duties, royal patronage, and oversight of government operations.

Contact: Palace of Rupertland, Capital District
Phone: 0800-PALACE
Email: palace@gov.rp

Ministry of the Interior

Minister: The Hon. Margaret Thornfield
Responsibilities: Citizen services, vital records, immigration, law enforcement, and emergency services.

Departments:

  • Rupertland Police Service
  • Emergency Management Agency
  • Citizenship and Immigration
  • Vital Records Office

Ministry of Finance

Minister: The Hon. Dr. James Richardson
Responsibilities: Government budget, taxation, benefits, economic policy, and financial regulation.

Departments:

  • Revenue Agency
  • Benefits Administration
  • Economic Development Office
  • Financial Services Authority

Ministry of Health and Social Services

Minister: The Hon. Dr. Sarah Chen
Responsibilities: Healthcare system, public health, social care, and disability services.

Departments:

  • Rupertland Health Service
  • Public Health Agency
  • Social Care Authority
  • Mental Health Commission

Ministry of Education and Skills

Minister: The Hon. David McKenzie
Responsibilities: Schools, universities, adult education, and skills training programs.

Departments:

  • School Education Authority
  • Higher Education Council
  • Skills Development Agency
  • Libraries and Culture Office

Ministry of Environment and Sustainability

Minister: The Hon. Lisa Greenwood
Responsibilities: Environmental protection, climate policy, conservation, and sustainable development.

Departments:

  • Environmental Protection Agency
  • Parks and Wildlife Service
  • Climate Change Office
  • Renewable Energy Authority

Ministry of Transport and Infrastructure

Minister: The Hon. Robert Stone
Responsibilities: Roads, public transport, vehicle licensing, and infrastructure development.

Departments:

  • Highway Authority
  • Public Transport Corporation
  • Vehicle Licensing Office
  • Infrastructure Development Agency

Specialized agencies

Rupertland Business Registry

Independent agency responsible for business registration, licensing, and commercial regulation.

Tourism Rupertland

Promotes tourism and manages visitor services, working with private sector partners to develop the tourism industry.

Rupertland Statistics Office

Collects and publishes official statistics on population, economy, and government performance.

Digital Government Office

Manages government IT systems, digital services, and cybersecurity across all departments.

Independent oversight

Ombudsman Office

Independent office that investigates complaints about government services and administration. Citizens can file complaints free of charge.

Audit Office

Independent auditor of government finances and performance, reporting directly to the Legislative Assembly.

Privacy Commissioner

Protects citizen privacy rights and oversees government data collection and information sharing practices.

Organizational structure

The government operates under a Westminster-style system with the Prince as head of state and a Legislative Assembly elected every four years. Ministers are appointed by the Prince on advice of the Premier, who leads the party with majority support in the Assembly.

Current government

Premier: The Hon. Elizabeth Harrington
Deputy Premier: The Hon. Michael Torres
Cabinet: 8 ministers plus Premier and Deputy Premier
Assembly: 25 elected members representing 5 districts
