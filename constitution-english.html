CONSTITUTION OF THE PRINCIPALITY OF RUPERTLAND

Official English Version

Enacted 15 March 1867, Last Amended 12 June 2023

PREAMBLE

We, the people of Rupertland, in order to form a more perfect union of our historic principality, establish justice, ensure domestic tranquility, provide for the common defense, promote the general welfare, and secure the blessings of liberty to ourselves and our posterity, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Principality of Rupertland.

PART I - FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

Article 1 - The State

The Principality of Rupertland is a sovereign, democratic constitutional monarchy founded on the rule of law and respect for human dignity.

Article 2 - Territory

The territory of Rupertland comprises the traditional lands granted by royal charter in 1670, including all islands, territorial waters, and airspace as defined by international law.

Article 3 - Official Languages

English is the official language of Rupertland. French and German shall be recognized as heritage languages with special status in education and cultural preservation.

PART II - FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS AND FREEDOMS

Article 4 - Human Dignity

Human dignity shall be inviolable. To respect and protect it shall be the duty of all state authority.

Article 5 - Right to Life and Liberty

Every person has the right to life and personal liberty. These rights may be interfered with only pursuant to a law.

Article 6 - Equality Before the Law

All persons shall be equal before the law. No person shall be discriminated against because of sex, parentage, race, language, homeland and origin, faith, or religious or political opinions.

Article 7 - Freedom of Expression

Every person shall have the right to freely express and disseminate his opinions in speech, writing, and pictures and to inform himself without hindrance from generally accessible sources.

Article 8 - Freedom of Assembly

All persons shall have the right to assemble peacefully and unarmed without prior notification or permission.

Article 9 - Freedom of Association

All persons shall have the right to form corporations and other associations.

Article 10 - Privacy of Communications

The privacy of correspondence, posts, and telecommunications shall be inviolable.

PART III - THE HEAD OF STATE

Article 11 - The Prince

The Prince is the Head of State. The succession follows the principle of primogeniture in the male line and, subsidiarily, in the female line, in the order of birth.

Article 12 - Powers of the Prince

The Prince:

  • Represents the State in its relations with foreign states
  • Signs and ratifies international treaties
  • Appoints diplomatic representatives
  • Grants pardons and commutes sentences
  • Confers titles and honors

Article 13 - Regency

If the Prince is unable to exercise his governmental duties, regency shall be exercised according to the House Law of the Princely Family.

PART IV - THE PARLIAMENT

Article 14 - Composition

The Parliament consists of 25 members elected by universal, equal, direct, and secret suffrage for a term of four years.

Article 15 - Electoral System

Members of Parliament are elected according to the principle of proportional representation. Every citizen who has attained the age of 18 years has the right to vote.

Article 16 - Legislative Powers

The Parliament exercises the legislative power. It passes laws, approves the state budget, and exercises parliamentary control over the Government.

Article 17 - Sessions

The Parliament convenes in ordinary session once a year. The Prince or one-third of the members may convene extraordinary sessions.

PART V - THE GOVERNMENT

Article 18 - Composition

The Government consists of the Head of Government and up to four additional members appointed by the Prince on the proposal of Parliament.

Article 19 - Powers and Duties

The Government:

  • Prepares legislation for Parliament
  • Implements laws and parliamentary resolutions
  • Administers the state budget
  • Represents the country in the European Union

Article 20 - Responsibility

The Government is responsible to Parliament. Parliament may pass a vote of no confidence by a majority of its members.

PART VI - THE COURTS

Article 21 - Judicial Independence

Judicial power shall be vested in independent courts. Judges shall be independent and subject only to the law.

Article 22 - Court Structure

The court system comprises:

  • The Constitutional Court
  • The Court of Appeal
  • The Regional Court
  • The Magistrates' Courts

Article 23 - Constitutional Court

The Constitutional Court decides on the constitutionality of laws and resolves constitutional disputes between state organs.

PART VII - CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

Article 24 - Amendment Procedure

This Constitution may be amended by a law passed by a two-thirds majority of Parliament and approved by the Prince.

Article 25 - Limitations

No amendment may alter the fundamental democratic and constitutional character of the State or the basic rights enumerated in Part II.

FINAL PROVISIONS

Article 26 - Entry into Force

This Constitution enters into force on the day of its promulgation by the Prince.

Article 27 - Previous Law

All previous constitutional laws are hereby repealed, except as specifically provided by transitional arrangements.

Official Seal of the Principality of Rupertland

Enacted by His Serene Highness Prince Rupert VII on 15 March 1867

Last amended by His Serene Highness Prince Rupert VIII on 12 June 2023
