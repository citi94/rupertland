Government transparency

The Principality of Rupertland is committed to open, transparent government. Access public data, government spending information, and exercise your right to information.

Open data portal

Government datasets

Hundreds of government datasets are freely available for download and use. All data is provided in open formats and updated regularly.

Popular datasets

  • Government spending and contracts - Updated monthly
  • Population and demographic statistics - Updated annually
  • Environmental monitoring data - Updated daily
  • Education and health statistics - Updated quarterly
  • Transport and infrastructure data - Updated monthly

Using government data

Government data is available under the Open Government License, allowing free use for any purpose including commercial applications. Attribution to the source is required.

Government spending

Budget transparency

The annual government budget is published in full with detailed breakdowns of all spending by department and program.

2024 Budget highlights

  • Total budget: RP$2.8 billion
  • Health and social services: 35% (RP$980 million)
  • Education: 25% (RP$700 million)
  • Infrastructure: 15% (RP$420 million)
  • Environment and sustainability: 10% (RP$280 million)
  • Other government operations: 15% (RP$420 million)

Contract transparency

All government contracts over RP$25,000 are published online within 30 days of signing. Information includes contractor details, contract value, and scope of work.

Recent major contracts

  • Electric bus fleet procurement - RP$15 million
  • Hospital equipment upgrade - RP$8.5 million
  • School construction project - RP$12 million
  • IT systems modernization - RP$6.2 million

Senior executive compensation

Salaries and benefits for all senior government officials are published annually. This includes ministers, deputy ministers, and agency heads.

Freedom of information

Your right to information

Citizens have the legal right to access government information under the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act.

How to make a request

  1. Submit request online or in writing
  2. Describe the information you're seeking clearly
  3. Pay application fee of RP$10 (waived for personal information)
  4. Receive response within 30 days

What information can I request?

  • Government policies and decision-making processes
  • Meeting minutes and correspondence
  • Research and analysis reports
  • Your own personal information held by government
  • Government studies and evaluations

Exemptions and limitations

Some information may be withheld to protect:

  • Personal privacy of other individuals
  • Cabinet confidentiality
  • Law enforcement investigations
  • Commercial confidentiality
  • National security (limited scope in Rupertland)

Make a freedom of information request

Online: FOI Request Portal
Email: foi@gov.rp
Phone: 0800-FREEDOM
Mail: FOI Coordinator, Government Complex

Performance reporting

Government performance dashboard

Real-time dashboard showing key government performance indicators including:

  • Service delivery wait times
  • Budget variance and spending rates
  • Environmental targets progress
  • Health system performance
  • Education outcomes

Annual reporting

Each government department publishes an annual report detailing:

  • Achievement of objectives and targets
  • Financial performance and budget outcomes
  • Key challenges and lessons learned
  • Plans for the following year

Parliamentary oversight

Legislative Assembly proceedings

All Legislative Assembly sessions are live-streamed and recorded. Transcripts and voting records are published within 48 hours.

Committee hearings

Parliamentary committees conduct public hearings on policy issues, government performance, and proposed legislation. Public can attend or submit written input.

Question period

Weekly question period allows Assembly members to question ministers about government activities and policies. Sessions are broadcast live.

Citizen engagement

Public consultations

Government conducts public consultations on major policy decisions. All consultation papers and public feedback summaries are published online.

Citizen advisory committees

Various advisory committees include citizen representatives to provide input on specific policy areas including health, education, and environment.

Open government partnership

Rupertland participates in international open government initiatives and regularly reports on transparency and accountability commitments.
