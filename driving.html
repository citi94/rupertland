Driving and transport

Information about driving licenses, vehicle registration, public transport, and sustainable transport options in the Principality of Rupertland.

Driving licenses

Getting your first license

You can apply for a learner's permit at age 16 and a full license at age 18. The process includes theory test, practical driving test, and vision assessment.

  • Theory test: RP$25 (includes highway code and hazard perception)
  • Practical test: RP$75 (45-minute driving assessment)
  • License fee: RP$50 (valid for 10 years)
  • Eye test required (free at testing centres)

License categories

  • Class 1: Cars and light vehicles up to 3.5 tonnes
  • Class 2: Heavy vehicles and trucks
  • Class 3: Motorcycles and scooters
  • Class 4: Buses and passenger vehicles
  • Class 5: Specialized vehicles (crane, forklift, etc.)

International visitors

Visitors can drive with a valid international driving permit for up to 6 months. Residents must obtain a Rupertland license within 3 months of arrival.

Vehicle registration

New vehicle registration

All vehicles must be registered within 14 days of purchase or import. Registration includes safety inspection and emissions testing.

  • Registration fee: RP$100 annually
  • Safety inspection: RP$75 (required annually)
  • Emissions test: RP$50 (required for vehicles over 3 years old)
  • License plates: RP$25 (one-time fee)

Electric vehicle incentives

Electric and hybrid vehicles receive significant incentives:

  • 50% reduction in registration fees
  • Free access to government charging stations
  • Purchase rebate up to RP$8,000
  • Free installation of home charging points

Public transport

Rupertland Transit System

Free electric bus service connecting all communities with services every 15-30 minutes during peak hours. Real-time tracking available through the RTS mobile app.

Inter-city rail service

Daily electric train service between major towns. Tickets from RP$5-15 depending on distance. Seniors, students, and disabled passengers travel free.

Ferry services

Regular ferry connections to outlying islands and coastal communities. Vehicle transport available on larger ferries.

Road safety

Speed limits

  • Urban areas: 50 km/h
  • School zones: 30 km/h
  • Rural roads: 80 km/h
  • Highways: 100 km/h

Safety regulations

Rupertland has strict safety laws to protect all road users:

  • Zero tolerance for drinking and driving
  • Mandatory seatbelts for all occupants
  • Hands-free phone use only while driving
  • Mandatory helmets for cyclists and motorcyclists
  • Winter tires required December-March

Penalties

Driving violations carry serious penalties including fines, license suspension, and vehicle impoundment for serious offenses.

Active transport

Cycling infrastructure

Extensive network of protected bike lanes and multi-use paths connecting residential areas to schools, workplaces, and shopping centres.

Bike share program

Free bike share with electric bikes available at 50 stations across urban areas. Register online or through the mobile app.

Walking and hiking

Comprehensive sidewalk network with accessible paths. Extensive trail system for recreation and commuting.

Commercial transport

Commercial vehicle licensing

Special licenses and permits required for commercial vehicles, taxis, and delivery services. Regular safety inspections and driver qualification testing mandatory.

Freight and shipping

Port facilities and freight rail connections support regional trade. Cargo bike delivery encouraged in urban areas with special licensing available.

Transport services

Book driving tests, register vehicles, or get transport information:

  • Online: Transport Portal
  • Phone: 0800-TRANSPORT
  • In person: Any Citizens Service Centre
! Warning Driving without a valid license or with an unregistered vehicle may result in vehicle impoundment and substantial fines.
