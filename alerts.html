Types of Alerts

Emergency Categories

CRITICAL ALERTS

Immediate threat to life or safety requiring urgent action

  • Severe weather warnings (storms, flooding)
  • Security threats or evacuation orders
  • Public health emergencies
  • Infrastructure failures (power, communications)
  • Chemical or environmental hazards

Delivery: Immediate SMS, email, and mobile app notifications

HIGH PRIORITY ALERTS

Important government announcements requiring prompt attention

  • Major policy changes affecting citizens
  • Service disruptions or closures
  • Travel advisories and border changes
  • Health alerts and public safety notices

Delivery: SMS and email within 30 minutes

STANDARD ALERTS

General government notifications and updates

  • New service launches
  • Planned maintenance notifications
  • Cultural events and public information
  • Consultation opportunities

Delivery: Email notifications (next business day)

Alert System Information

How Alerts Work

  1. Emergency Management Office identifies potential threat or urgent situation
  2. Alert content is prepared in appropriate languages
  3. Alert is distributed via SMS, email, and mobile app simultaneously
  4. Follow-up information posted on government website and social media
  5. All-clear notification sent when situation is resolved

Privacy and Data Protection

  • Contact information used only for emergency alerts
  • No sharing with third parties or commercial use
  • Unsubscribe at any time via website or reply "STOP" to SMS
  • Data stored securely in encrypted government systems
  • Regular deletion of inactive accounts
