Parliament of Rupertland

The Parliament of Rupertland is the democratic heart of our nation, where elected representatives debate policy, pass laws, and hold the government accountable to the people.

How Parliament Works

Parliamentary Structure

  • 12 elected Members of Parliament (MPs) representing all citizens
  • 4-year terms with elections held every four years
  • Universal suffrage for all citizens aged 16 and over
  • Proportional representation system ensuring fair representation
  • Speaker of Parliament maintains order and ensures proper procedures

Parliamentary Functions

  • Legislative: Debate, amend, and pass laws for Rupertland
  • Oversight: Hold the government accountable through questions and committees
  • Budget approval: Review and approve government spending plans
  • Representation: Represent the interests and concerns of citizens
  • Constitutional role: Participate in constitutional amendments and reviews

Legislative Process

How a Bill Becomes Law

  1. Bill Introduction: Government or any MP introduces a proposed law
  2. First Reading: Bill title and general purpose announced to Parliament
  3. Second Reading: Detailed debate on the bill's principles and objectives
  4. Committee Stage: Line-by-line examination and amendment proposals
  5. Report Stage: Parliament considers committee recommendations
  6. Third Reading: Final debate and voting on the completed bill
  7. Royal Assent: Prince Rupert signs the bill into law
  8. Commencement: Law comes into effect on the specified date

Parliamentary Sessions

Session Schedule

  • Regular sessions: Tuesdays and Thursdays, 2:00 PM - 6:00 PM
  • Question Time: First 30 minutes of each session
  • Committee meetings: Wednesdays, 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • Special sessions: Called as needed for urgent matters
  • Summer recess: August 1-31 (emergency sessions if required)

Attending Parliament

Citizens can observe parliamentary sessions from the public gallery. Sessions are also broadcast live online and on television.

Public Gallery

  • Capacity: 40 seats available for public viewing
  • Booking: Book online or call +41 555 PARL (7275)
  • Security: Valid ID required, security screening
  • Guidelines: No photography, phones on silent, respectful behavior
  • Tours: Guided tours available Monday, Wednesday, Friday

Parliamentary Records

Hansard

Complete transcripts of all parliamentary proceedings are published as Hansard, the official parliamentary record.

  • Daily Hansard: Published within 24 hours of each session
  • Searchable database: All records since 2019 available online
  • Languages: Available in English and French
  • Access: Free download or subscription service
