Legal Aid

Free legal advice and representation for people who cannot afford a lawyer in the Principality of Rupertland.

Constitutional right: Every person in Rupertland has the right to legal representation. If you cannot afford a lawyer, the state will provide one at no cost.

Who Can Get Legal Aid

Eligibility Criteria

Legal aid is available to anyone who meets the financial eligibility requirements and has a legal matter that qualifies for assistance.

Financial Eligibility (2025)

  • Single person: Monthly income below 3,500 CHF
  • Couple: Combined monthly income below 5,200 CHF
  • Each dependent child: Additional 800 CHF allowance
  • Assets: Savings and property below 15,000 CHF (excluding primary residence)

Emergency cases: Income limits may be waived in urgent situations involving constitutional rights or serious criminal charges.

Qualifying Legal Matters

Criminal Law

  • Criminal defense representation
  • Appeals of criminal convictions
  • Victims' rights assistance
  • Juvenile justice cases

Note: Legal aid for criminal defense is guaranteed regardless of income for serious charges.

Family Law

  • Divorce and separation proceedings
  • Child custody and support disputes
  • Domestic violence protection orders
  • Adoption and guardianship matters
  • Child protection cases

Immigration Law

  • Asylum and refugee applications
  • Deportation and removal proceedings
  • Immigration appeals
  • Citizenship applications (complex cases)

Constitutional and Human Rights

  • Constitutional rights violations
  • Discrimination cases
  • Freedom of expression and assembly
  • Privacy and data protection rights
  • Access to government services

Other Civil Matters

  • Housing disputes (evictions, habitability)
  • Employment law (wrongful termination, discrimination)
  • Consumer protection issues
  • Administrative law appeals
  • Social benefits disputes

Types of Legal Aid Services

Full Representation

A qualified lawyer will represent you throughout your case, including:

  • Legal research and case preparation
  • Court appearances and oral arguments
  • Negotiating settlements
  • Filing legal documents and appeals
  • Ongoing case management

Limited Scope Assistance

Help with specific aspects of your legal matter:

  • Document review and preparation
  • Legal advice and consultation
  • Brief court appearances
  • Settlement negotiations
  • Self-representation coaching

How to Apply

Application Process

  1. Contact the Legal Aid Office by phone, email, or in person
  2. Initial screening to determine if your case qualifies
  3. Complete application form with required documentation
  4. Financial assessment to verify eligibility
  5. Case assignment to appropriate lawyer or clinic
  6. Begin legal assistance within 5 business days
! Warning Apply as soon as possible. Legal deadlines can pass quickly, and waiting may harm your case.

Required Documents

Bring the following documents when applying:

  • Identity: ID card, passport, or residence permit
  • Income: Pay stubs, employment letter, social benefits statements
  • Assets: Bank statements, property documents, investment records
  • Legal matter: Court papers, letters from lawyers, relevant contracts
  • Family: Marriage certificate, children's birth certificates (if applicable)

Emergency Legal Aid

Urgent Situations

Emergency legal aid is available 24/7 for urgent matters:

  • Arrest or police detention
  • Domestic violence protection orders
  • Emergency child custody issues
  • Immediate deportation proceedings
  • Constitutional rights violations

Legal Aid for Special Groups

Refugees and Asylum Seekers

Specialized legal aid services for refugees and asylum seekers:

  • Asylum application assistance
  • Refugee status determination appeals
  • Family reunification proceedings
  • Translation and interpretation services
  • Cultural mediators available

Youth Under 18

All persons under 18 automatically qualify for legal aid regardless of family income:

  • Juvenile criminal defense
  • Child protection proceedings
  • Education rights advocacy
  • Emancipation proceedings
  • Immigration matters affecting minors

Persons with Disabilities

Enhanced legal aid services for persons with disabilities:

  • Disability rights and accommodation
  • Benefits and support services
  • Guardianship and capacity issues
  • Accessible communication support
  • Sign language interpreters provided

Alternative Dispute Resolution

Mediation Services

Free mediation for eligible disputes:

  • Family disputes (divorce, custody, support)
  • Landlord-tenant conflicts
  • Neighbor disputes
  • Consumer complaints
  • Employment disputes

Mediation is often faster and less stressful than court proceedings, and helps preserve relationships.

Quality and Complaints

Our Standards

All legal aid lawyers are qualified and experienced practitioners who meet strict quality standards:

  • Minimum 3 years practice experience
  • Specialized training in legal aid practice
  • Regular continuing education requirements
  • Performance monitoring and quality assurance
  • Client satisfaction surveys

Making a Complaint

If you are not satisfied with your legal aid service:

  1. First, speak directly with your lawyer or clinic supervisor
  2. If unresolved, contact the Legal Aid Director
  3. File a formal complaint with the Bar Association if necessary
  4. Contact the Ombudsman for systemic issues

Complaints are taken seriously and investigated promptly. You will not lose your legal aid for making a legitimate complaint.
