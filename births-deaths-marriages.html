Births, deaths and marriages

Register life events and obtain official certificates for births, deaths, marriages, and civil partnerships in the Principality of Rupertland.

Birth registration

Registering a birth

All births in Rupertland must be registered within 42 days. Registration is free and can be done online, by post, or in person at any Vital Records Office.

  • Registration must be done by a parent or legal guardian
  • Hospital births are often pre-registered by medical staff
  • Home births require additional medical documentation
  • Both parents' information is recorded if available

Required information

  • Child's full name and date of birth
  • Place of birth (hospital or address)
  • Parents' full names, dates of birth, and occupations
  • Parents' place of birth and citizenship
  • Medical certificate of birth (from attending physician)

Birth certificates

Official birth certificates are available immediately after registration. First certificate is free, additional copies cost RP$15 each.

Marriage and civil partnerships

Getting married in Rupertland

Marriage is legal for all couples regardless of gender. You must give 30 days notice before your ceremony and be aged 18 or over (or 16-17 with parental consent).

Notice of intended marriage

Both parties must appear in person at a Vital Records Office to give notice:

  • Notice fee: RP$75 per person
  • Valid for 12 months after 30-day waiting period
  • Requires proof of identity and eligibility to marry
  • Previous marriages must be legally ended

Marriage ceremony

Ceremonies can be civil (at government offices) or religious (at registered venues):

  • Civil ceremony fee: RP$150
  • Must have 2 witnesses aged 18 or over
  • Ceremony conducted by authorized registrar or clergy
  • Marriage certificate issued immediately

Civil partnerships

Civil partnerships provide the same legal rights as marriage. Same process and fees apply as for marriage registration.

Death registration

Registering a death

Deaths must be registered within 5 days. Registration is typically handled by funeral directors, but family members can register directly.

Required documents

  • Medical certificate of death (from doctor or coroner)
  • Deceased's birth certificate or passport
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Information about deceased's occupation and residence

Death certificates

Death certificates are needed for settling estate matters, insurance claims, and other legal purposes. First certificate is free, additional copies cost RP$15.

Divorce and dissolution

Divorce proceedings

Divorce applications are processed through the Family Court. No-fault divorce is available after 12 months of separation.

Civil partnership dissolution

Civil partnerships can be dissolved through the same court process as divorce. Mediation services are available to help resolve disputes.

Certificates and records

Ordering certificates

Certificates can be ordered online, by phone, or in person:

  • Standard processing: 5 business days
  • Express service: Next business day (additional RP$25)
  • Postal delivery included in fee
  • International delivery available

Certificate types

  • Full certificate: All recorded information (RP$15)
  • Short certificate: Basic details only (RP$10)
  • Commemorative certificate: Special presentation format (RP$25)
  • Certified copies: For legal purposes (RP$20)

Historical records

Vital records dating back to 1825 are available. Genealogical research services provided by the Archives Office.

Vital Records Offices

Main Office: Government Complex, Capital District
Hours: Monday-Friday 8:30am-4:30pm
Phone: 0800-RECORDS
Regional offices in all major towns

Online services: Citizen Portal

Name changes

Adults can legally change their name through a statutory declaration process. Fee of RP$100 applies. Children's names can be changed with parental consent or court order.

! Important Failing to register births or deaths within required timeframes may result in penalties. Contact the Vital Records Office if you need assistance with late registration.
