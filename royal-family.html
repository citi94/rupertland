The Royal Family

The Royal Family of Rupertland serves as the constitutional head of state, providing continuity, stability, and representation for the Principality.

Constitutional Role: Under the Constitution of Rupertland, the Crown serves as Head of State in a parliamentary democracy, with executive power exercised by the democratically elected government.

His Serene Highness Prince Rupert Harding

Head of State and Founding Prince

Born: March 15, 1985 (age 40)
Reign: Since founding of Rupertland (2018)
Full title: His Serene Highness Prince Rupert Harding, Sovereign Prince of Rupertland
Residence: Rupert Castle, New Rupertville

Background and Early Life

Prince Rupert was born in Cambridge, England, and raised with a strong commitment to environmental sustainability and technological innovation. His vision of creating a modern, environmentally conscious principality led to the foundation of Rupertland in 2018.

Education

  • PhD in Environmental Science - Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), 2012
  • MBA - Stanford Graduate School of Business, 2009
  • BA in Natural Sciences - Trinity College, Cambridge, 2007
  • International Baccalaureate - United World College Atlantic, 2003

Professional Experience

  • 2012-2018: Chief Technology Officer, GreenTech Solutions International
  • 2014-2017: Climate Policy Advisor, United Nations Environment Programme
  • 2010-2012: Research Fellow, MIT Climate Change Initiative
  • 2009-2010: Management Consultant, McKinsey & Company

Constitutional Role and Duties

Head of State Functions

As constitutional monarch, Prince Rupert performs important ceremonial and constitutional duties:

  • Royal Assent: Signing laws passed by Parliament into legal effect
  • Government Formation: Appointing the Prime Minister and Cabinet
  • State Representation: Representing Rupertland in international relations
  • Constitutional Guardian: Ensuring constitutional procedures are followed
  • National Unity: Serving as a unifying figure above party politics

Annual State Opening of Parliament

Each year, Prince Rupert delivers the Throne Speech at the opening of Parliament, outlining the government's legislative program for the coming session. This ceremony symbolizes the partnership between Crown and Parliament in Rupertland's democracy.

Prince Rupert is a passionate advocate for environmental sustainability and climate action:

  • Patron: Rupertland Environmental Foundation

Under his leadership, Rupertland has committed to achieving carbon neutrality by 2030 and has become a model for sustainable development in small nations.

  • Open Government: Promoting transparency and citizen engagement through technology

Succession and Future

Constitutional Succession

The Constitution of Rupertland establishes clear succession rules based on absolute primogeniture, meaning the first-born child, regardless of gender, inherits the throne. The succession is also subject to parliamentary approval and constitutional requirements.

As Prince Rupert is currently unmarried and has no children, succession planning remains part of ongoing constitutional discussions involving Parliament and legal experts.

Modernizing the Monarchy

Prince Rupert has implemented several reforms to modernize the constitutional monarchy:

  • Transparency: Regular publication of royal finances and activities
  • Accessibility: Increased public access to royal events and residences
  • Environmental Leadership: Making royal operations carbon-neutral
  • Digital Engagement: Active social media presence and online interactions
  • Constitutional Reform: Supporting democratic governance and citizen participation
