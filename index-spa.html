About the Principality of Rupertland

A modern constitutional monarchy committed to digital innovation, environmental sustainability, and citizen welfare in the heart of Europe.

Our Story

Founded in 2018 by Prince Rupert Harding, the Principality of Rupertland represents a new model of governance that combines traditional constitutional monarchy with cutting-edge digital government and unwavering commitment to environmental protection.

Key Facts

  • Population: 4,750 residents
  • Area: 12.3 square kilometers
  • Capital: New Rupertville
  • Languages: English (official), French (official)
  • Currency: Swiss Franc (CHF)
  • Time Zone: Central European Time (CET)
  • Government: Constitutional monarchy with parliamentary democracy

Government Structure

Head of State

His Serene Highness Prince Rupert Harding serves as constitutional monarch and Head of State, with ceremonial duties and constitutional responsibilities.

Immigration Services

Visa applications, residence permits, work permits, and citizenship pathways for the Principality of Rupertland.

New arrivals: All visitors must present valid identification at border control. EU/EEA citizens can stay for 90 days without a visa.

Visitor Visas

Tourist Visa (30 days)

  • Fee: 60 CHF
  • Processing: 5-10 working days
  • Requirements: Valid passport, accommodation proof, return ticket

Business Visa (90 days)

  • Fee: 120 CHF
  • Processing: 10-15 working days
  • Requirements: Business invitation, company registration, financial proof

Residence Permits

Temporary Residence (B Permit)

  • Valid for 1 year, renewable
  • Work authorization included
  • Path to permanent residence
  • Fee: 350 CHF

Permanent Residence (C Permit)

  • Valid for 5 years, renewable
  • Unrestricted work rights
  • Access to social benefits
  • Fee: 500 CHF

Work Permits

Skilled Worker Permit

For professionals with university qualifications or specialized skills

  • Job offer from Rupertland employer required
  • Salary minimum: 75,000 CHF annually
  • Processing time: 4-8 weeks
  • Fee: 400 CHF

Entrepreneur Permit

For investors and business founders

  • Minimum investment: 250,000 CHF
  • Business plan approval required
  • Job creation commitment: 3+ positions
  • Fee: 800 CHF

Citizenship

Naturalization Requirements

  • Residence: 10 years continuous residence (5 years with EU citizenship)
  • Language: B2 level in English or French
  • Integration: Pass civic knowledge test
  • Character: Clean criminal record

Residence Permit Application

Apply for temporary or permanent residence in the Principality of Rupertland.

! Warning Applications must be submitted at least 30 days before your current visa expires.

Permit Types

Temporary Residence (B Permit)

  • Duration: 1-5 years, renewable
  • Eligibility: Employment, family reunification, studies
  • Fee: 165 CHF
  • Processing time: 6-8 weeks

Required documents:

  • Valid passport with entry stamp
  • Completed application form (RP-101)
  • Employment contract or university acceptance letter
  • Accommodation confirmation
  • Health insurance certificate
  • Criminal background check from home country
  • Passport photos (2x, biometric standard)

Permanent Residence (C Permit)

  • Duration: Indefinite
  • Eligibility: 5+ years continuous residence
  • Fee: 350 CHF
  • Processing time: 10-12 weeks

Eligibility requirements:

  • 5 years continuous residence with B permit
  • Basic Rupertlandic language proficiency (Level A2)
  • No serious criminal convictions
  • Integration demonstrated through community involvement
  • Financial self-sufficiency

Short-term Work Permit (L Permit)

  • Duration: Up to 1 year
  • Eligibility: Temporary employment contracts
  • Fee: 90 CHF
  • Processing time: 3-4 weeks

Conditions:

  • Tied to specific employer and position
  • Cannot be extended beyond 12 months
  • Must transition to B permit for longer stays
  • Limited to EU/EEA citizens and skilled professionals

Application Process

  1. Prepare documents - Gather all required documentation based on permit type
  2. Book appointment - Schedule appointment at Residence Office online or by phone
  3. Submit application - Attend appointment with complete application and pay fees
  4. Biometric data - Provide fingerprints and digital photograph
  5. Wait for decision - Processing times vary by permit type and complexity
  6. Collect permit - Pick up residence permit card in person with ID
Important: Your residence permit card must be carried at all times while in Rupertland. Digital copies are not accepted by law enforcement.

Legal Aid

Free legal advice and representation for people who cannot afford a lawyer in the Principality of Rupertland.

Constitutional right: Every person in Rupertland has the right to legal representation. If you cannot afford a lawyer, the state will provide one at no cost.

Eligibility

Financial Eligibility (2025)

  • Single person: Monthly income below 3,500 CHF
  • Couple: Combined monthly income below 5,200 CHF
  • Each dependent child: Additional 800 CHF allowance
  • Assets: Savings and property below 15,000 CHF

Services Available

Criminal Law

  • Criminal defense representation
  • Appeals of criminal convictions
  • Victims' rights assistance
  • Juvenile justice cases

Family Law

  • Divorce and separation proceedings
  • Child custody and support disputes
  • Domestic violence protection orders
  • Adoption and guardianship matters

Emergency Legal Aid Hotline

Call +41 555 LEGAL (53425)
Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week

How to Apply

  1. Contact the Legal Aid Office by phone, email, or in person
  2. Initial screening to determine if your case qualifies
  3. Complete application form with required documentation
  4. Financial assessment to verify eligibility
  5. Case assignment to appropriate lawyer or clinic
  6. Begin legal assistance within 5 business days

Parliament of Rupertland

The Parliament of Rupertland is the democratic heart of our nation, where elected representatives debate policy, pass laws, and hold the government accountable to the people.

How Parliament Works

Parliamentary Structure

  • 12 elected Members of Parliament (MPs) representing all citizens
  • 4-year terms with elections held every four years
  • Universal suffrage for all citizens aged 16 and over
  • Proportional representation system ensuring fair representation
  • Speaker of Parliament maintains order and ensures proper procedures

Parliamentary Functions

  • Legislative: Debate, amend, and pass laws for Rupertland
  • Oversight: Hold the government accountable through questions and committees
  • Budget approval: Review and approve government spending plans
  • Representation: Represent the interests and concerns of citizens
  • Constitutional role: Participate in constitutional amendments and reviews

Legislative Process

How Laws Are Made

  1. Bill Introduction: Government or Parliament member introduces proposed law
  2. First Reading: Bill title and general purpose announced
  3. Second Reading: Detailed debate on bill principles
  4. Committee Stage: Line-by-line examination and amendments
  5. Third Reading: Final debate and voting
  6. Royal Assent: Prince Rupert signs bill into law
  7. Commencement: Law comes into effect on specified date

Department structure

Each ministry is led by an elected Minister who is responsible for policy direction and oversight. Day-to-day operations are managed by permanent civil servants headed by a Permanent Secretary.

How departments work

  • Ministers: Set policy direction and represent departments in Parliament
  • Permanent Secretaries: Senior civil servants responsible for departmental management
  • Deputy Ministers: Support ministers with specific portfolio areas
  • Director Generals: Lead major departmental divisions and programs
  • Civil Service: Professional public servants who deliver government services

Constitution of the Principality of Rupertland

The Constitution of the Principality of Rupertland is the fundamental law establishing the governmental structure, citizens' rights, and basic principles of our nation.

Constitutional Facts

  • Adopted: March 15, 2019
  • Last amended: January 12, 2023 (Environmental Protection Enhancement)
  • Articles: 85 total
  • Languages: English and French (official versions)
  • Status: Supreme law of Rupertland

Download complete Constitution (PDF)

Constitutional Principle: "The Principality of Rupertland shall be a constitutional monarchy committed to democratic governance, environmental sustainability, digital innovation, and the welfare of all citizens." - Article 1

Structure of the Constitution

Part I: Fundamental Rights and Freedoms (Articles 1-20)

Core Rights Guaranteed:

  • Article 2: Right to life, liberty, and security of person
  • Article 3: Freedom of expression, assembly, and association
  • Article 4: Right to privacy and data protection
  • Article 5: Freedom of religion and conscience
  • Article 6: Right to equality and non-discrimination
  • Article 7: Right to democratic participation and voting
  • Article 8: Right to education and healthcare
  • Article 9: Right to clean environment and sustainable development
  • Article 10: Right to digital access and digital rights

Part II: Structure of Government (Articles 21-40)

Establishes the three branches of government: Executive (The Crown and Cabinet), Legislative (Parliament), and Judicial (Courts and tribunals).

  • Separation of Powers: Clear division of responsibilities between branches
  • Checks and Balances: Each branch can limit the powers of others
  • Rule of Law: All government action must be authorized by law
  • Constitutional Supremacy: Constitution is the highest law

Part III: Parliamentary Democracy (Articles 41-55)

Details the structure and powers of Parliament, electoral system, and democratic processes.

Key Democratic Principles:

  • Universal Suffrage: All citizens 16+ can vote
  • Proportional Representation: Electoral system ensuring fair representation
  • Parliamentary Sessions: Regular public sessions and transparency
  • Legislative Process: Democratic law-making procedures
  • Opposition Rights: Protected role of parliamentary opposition

Part IV: The Crown and Executive (Articles 56-65)

Defines the role of Prince Rupert as constitutional monarch and the powers of the executive branch.

  • Constitutional Monarchy: Prince serves as Head of State within constitutional limits
  • Cabinet Government: Prime Minister and Cabinet exercise executive power
  • Royal Assent: Prince's role in the legislative process
  • Emergency Powers: Limited emergency powers with parliamentary oversight

Part V: Judicial System (Articles 66-75)

Establishes an independent judiciary and court system to interpret laws and protect rights.

  • Judicial Independence: Courts free from political interference
  • Supreme Court: Highest court for constitutional and legal matters
  • Access to Justice: Right to fair trial and legal representation
  • Constitutional Review: Courts can review laws for constitutional compliance

Part VI: Environmental Protection (Articles 76-80)

Unique Feature: Rupertland's Constitution includes specific environmental protections, making it one of the first constitutions to enshrine environmental rights.

  • Right to Clean Environment: Constitutional right to healthy environment
  • Sustainability Mandate: Government must consider environmental impact
  • Climate Action: Constitutional requirement for climate change response
  • Future Generations: Duty to protect environment for future citizens
  • Green Economy: Mandate to develop sustainable economic practices

Part VII: Amendment Procedures (Articles 81-85)

Sets out how the Constitution can be amended to adapt to changing circumstances while maintaining core principles.

  • Parliamentary Approval: Two-thirds majority required in Parliament
  • Public Consultation: Mandatory consultation on major amendments
  • Referendum: Some amendments require citizen referendum
  • Protected Rights: Core rights cannot be amended without special procedures

Constitutional Amendments

Recent Amendment (2023)

Environmental Protection Enhancement Amendment

Enacted: January 12, 2023
Purpose: Strengthened environmental protections and added specific climate targets
Key Changes: Mandatory carbon neutrality by 2030, enhanced renewable energy requirements, stronger environmental assessment processes

Amendment Process

  1. Proposal: Amendment proposed by Parliament, Cabinet, or citizen petition (5,000+ signatures)
  2. Parliamentary Review: Constitutional Committee reviews proposal
  3. Public Consultation: 60-day public consultation period
  4. Parliamentary Vote: Two-thirds majority in Parliament required
  5. Royal Assent: Prince Rupert signs amendment into law
  6. Implementation: Amendment comes into effect on specified date

Constitutional Rights in Practice

The Constitution is not just a historical document - it actively protects your rights every day. If you believe your constitutional rights have been violated, you can seek remedy through the courts.

Enforcing Your Rights

  • Constitutional Challenges: Challenge laws or government actions in court
  • Human Rights Tribunal: Specialized tribunal for rights violations
  • Ombudsman: Independent office to investigate government conduct
  • Legal Aid: Free legal assistance for constitutional cases

Property Registry Services

Register property ownership, transfers, and search the official property register in Rupertland.

Digital first: Most property registry services are available online 24/7. Physical visits are only required for notarized document submission.

Services Available

Property Search

Search the official property register by address, parcel number, or owner name.

  • Fee: 25 CHF per search
  • Available: 24/7 online
  • Results: Instant digital certificate

Property Registration

Register new property ownership or update existing records.

  • Fee: 150-500 CHF
  • Processing: 5-10 working days
  • Required: Notarized documents

Ownership Transfer

Transfer property ownership through sale, inheritance, or gift.

  • Fee: 0.5% of property value
  • Processing: 10-15 working days
  • Tax: Transfer tax applies

Mortgage Registration

Register mortgage liens and security interests on properties.

  • Fee: 200 CHF flat fee
  • Processing: 3-5 working days
  • Valid: Until discharged

For Property Registration

Data Protection

Your personal data is protected by law in Rupertland. Learn about your rights, how organizations must handle your data, and how to file complaints.

Constitutional right: Article 4 of the Constitution guarantees every person's right to privacy and data protection. This fundamental right is protected by comprehensive data protection laws and independent oversight.

Your Data Protection Rights

Eight Fundamental Rights

Under the Rupertland Data Protection Act 2023, you have the following rights:

1. Right to be Informed

Organizations must tell you how they collect and use your personal data.

2. Right of Access

You can request a copy of your personal data, free of charge.

3. Right to Rectification

You can ask organizations to correct inaccurate personal data.

4. Right to Erasure

You can ask organizations to delete your personal data.

5. Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask organizations to limit how they use your data.

6. Right to Data Portability

You can ask for your data in a commonly used electronic format.

7. Right to Object

You can object to the use of your data for marketing or research.

8. Automated Decision-Making Rights

You have rights regarding automated decisions and profiling.

Filing a Complaint

Data Protection Authority

The Data Protection Authority (DPA) is the independent regulator responsible for enforcing data protection laws in Rupertland.

How to File a Complaint

  1. Try to resolve directly first: Contact the organization's data protection officer
  2. Gather evidence: Collect relevant documents, emails, and correspondence
  3. Submit complaint: Use our online form or write to the DPA
  4. Provide details: Explain what happened and what you want to achieve
  5. Await response: The DPA will acknowledge within 5 working days
