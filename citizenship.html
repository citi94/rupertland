Citizenship services

Apply for Rupertland citizenship, check your status, or access services for current citizens.

Apply for citizenship

You may be eligible for Rupertland citizenship if you:

  • Have been a legal resident for 5 years or more
  • Are married to a Rupertland citizen
  • Have Rupertland ancestry
  • Were born in Rupertland to non-citizen parents
  • Have been granted asylum or refugee status
Processing time: 6-12 months for standard applications. Priority processing available for certain categories.
Start your application

What you'll need

  • Valid passport or travel document
  • Birth certificate
  • Proof of residence in Rupertland
  • Employment or education records
  • Criminal background check
  • Language proficiency certificate (if required)
  • Application fee: RP 2,500

Types of citizenship

Citizenship by birth

You're automatically a citizen if you were born in Rupertland and at least one parent was a citizen or legal resident.

Citizenship by descent

You may be eligible if your parent or grandparent was a Rupertland citizen, even if you were born outside Rupertland.

Citizenship by naturalization

Apply after meeting residency requirements and demonstrating integration into Rupertland society.

Citizenship by marriage

Expedited process available for spouses of Rupertland citizens after 3 years of marriage and 2 years of residence.

Dual citizenship

Rupertland allows dual citizenship. You don't need to give up your current nationality to become a Rupertland citizen, and Rupertland citizens can acquire other nationalities.

Citizenship test

Most applicants must pass a citizenship test covering:

  • Rupertland history and culture
  • Government and democratic values
  • Rights and responsibilities of citizens
  • Geography and national symbols
  • Basic English or French language skills

Study materials and practice tests are available online and at community centers.

Renouncing citizenship

Citizens can renounce their Rupertland citizenship, but this is permanent and cannot be reversed. You must have another nationality before renouncing.
