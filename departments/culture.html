Ministry of Culture

Responsible for arts, heritage preservation, sports development, media regulation, and promoting cultural diversity across Rupertland.

Cultural announcement

Rupertland Arts Festival 2025 - applications now open. Apply for funding.

What we do

The Ministry of Culture enriches the cultural life of Rupertland through:

  • Arts funding and development programs
  • Heritage site protection and museum management
  • Sports facility development and Olympic programs
  • Broadcasting and media content regulation
  • Public library and archive services
  • Cultural education and community programs
  • International cultural exchange initiatives

Policies and priorities

Current priorities

  • Digital transformation of cultural institutions
  • Accessibility improvements across all venues
  • Youth engagement and education programs
  • International cultural exchange partnerships
  • Sustainable tourism and heritage protection

Strategic objectives (2024-2027)

  1. Increase cultural participation by 25% across all demographics
  2. Complete digitization of national archives and collections
  3. Achieve carbon neutrality for all cultural venues by 2026
  4. Establish Rupertland as a leading cultural tourism destination
  5. Launch Olympic preparation program for 2032 games bid

Cultural development

Rupertland Creative Quarter: A new cultural district featuring artist studios, performance spaces, and creative businesses opening in 2026.

Major initiatives
